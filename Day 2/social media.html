Social media are interactive technologies that facilitate the creation, sharing and aggregation of content, ideas, interests, and other forms of expression through virtual communities and networks.[1][2] Social media refers to new forms of media that involve interactive participation. While challenges to the definition of social media arise[3][4] due to the variety of stand-alone and built-in social media services currently available, there are some common features:[2] Social media apps are online platforms that enable users to create and share content and participate in social networking.[2][5][6] User-generated content—such as text posts or comments, digital photos or videos, and data generated through all online interactions—is the lifeblood of social media.[2][5] Users create service-specific profiles for the website or app that are designed and maintained by the social media organization.[2][7] Social media helps the development of online social networks by connecting a user's profile with those of other individuals or groups.[2][7] The term social in regard to media suggests that platforms are user-centric and enable communal activity.
